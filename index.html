Tow truck insurance Australia is specialized commercial vehicle insurance covering public liability, vehicle damage, and business risks specific to towing operations. It's essential because tow truck operators face unique risks including damage to customer vehicles (on-hook coverage), third-party property damage, and workplace injuries. Most Australian states legally require minimum public liability coverage for licensed tow truck businesses.

Yes, tow truck liability insurance is mandatory in most Australian states. Minimum public liability insurance is required for commercial towing licenses, typically ranging from $10-20 million coverage. Additional requirements include workers compensation for employees and specific endorsements for heavy vehicle operations depending on your state and local council regulations.

Public liability for tow truck operators covers third-party property damage and injury claims arising from your towing operations. This includes damage to other vehicles, buildings, or infrastructure during towing, accidents while maneuvering in tight spaces, and injuries to bystanders. It does not cover damage to vehicles being towed (that requires separate on-hook insurance).
